Introduction

The Baby Lanuage Tank, the brand new solution for your children!


The Baby Language Tank project, led by a team of linguists, psychologists, and engineers at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, aims to use easily administered tests to measure language development in babies and toddlers.

By employing these tests, the project can assess children's language skills and identify areas where they may need additional support. Number of Students with Special Educational Needs (language-related) in Public Sector Ordinary Primary and Secondary Schools has been increasing in the past five years. Based on the results, the team offers professional advice and guidance to parents, providing tailored recommendations to enhance language development.

This approach ensures that parents have access to valuable insights about their child's progress and can make informed decisions about their language learning journey.

Findings

Our expertise test for distinguishing your kids language level






Discuss with doctors or nurses if your child has the following problem(s) when he or she reaches the age:

By the end of the first month, your baby:

  • • Seldom moves her arms or legs
  • • Appears excessively floppy or stiff
  • • Does not blink to bright light
  • • Does not focus or follow your face briefly at near
  • • Does not respond to loud noises

By the end of the third month, your baby:

  • • Appears floppy or too stiff on handling
  • • Does not move much and not support her head temporarily when lying on her tummy
  • • Keeps her hands fisted all the time and does not hold rattle put in her own hands
  • • Does not follow moving objects with her eyes at near
  • • Does not respond to loud noises
  • • Does not make any sound
  • • Does not smile in response to your voice or face

By the end of the seventh month, your baby:

  • • Does not move much or move asymmetrically
  • • Does not sit well with support on her hands
  • • Does not bear some weight on legs when being held up
  • • Does not reach and grasp objects
  • • Does not visually follow objects nearby or at a distance
  • • Has one or both eyes consistently turn in or out (i.e. squinting)
  • • Does not respond to calling
  • • Does not turn head to locate the source of sound
  • • Does not vocalize

Language Test

Our language test is designed to assess the language development of babies and toddlers in a simple and user-friendly manner, providing parents with valuable insights into their child's language skills and identify any areas where additional support may be needed.

Cognition Test

Cognitive performance, which is closely related to language development, is an important index of child development. This webpage provides cognitive tests and games.

Sensorimotor Test

By simply observing behaviors, we can gain insight into the child’s development. If a child's behavior does not align with expectations in certain aspects of development, it is recommended to provide follow-up attention.
